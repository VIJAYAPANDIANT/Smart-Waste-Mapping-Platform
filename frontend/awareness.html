Waste Segregation Guide

Proper segregation at source is the first step towards a sustainable future.

Organic Waste

Biodegradable waste that can be composted.

  • Fruit & Vegetable peels
  • Leftover food
  • Garden waste (leaves, grass)
  • Eggshells

Dry Waste

Non-biodegradable but recyclable materials.

  • Paper & Cardboard
  • Plastic bottles & containers
  • Metal cans
  • Glass jars

E-Waste / Hazardous

Requires special handling and disposal.

  • Batteries & Bulbs
  • Old electronics
  • Paint & Chemicals
  • Medical waste

Easy Recycling Tips

  • Rinse before recycling: Food residue can contaminate entire batches of recyclables.
  • Say no to plastic bags: They clog recycling machinery. Use reusable bags instead.
  • Flatten boxes: It saves space in recycling bins and transportation trucks.
